# DIGITAL-LITERACY--PROJECT
This project focuses on building digital literacy and responsible online behavior among students. It includes creating an infographic, building a digital portfolio, exploring coding and collaboration tools, drafting professional emails, and understanding cyber safety through case studies and prevention tips for safe digital practices.

## Project Overview
This project is something I am working on as a Student Digital Ambassador. It is about learning how to use technology and the internet in an good way. The project has five parts that cover important things like being good at using computers having a good online personality sending emails and knowing about cybercrime.

The project shows us why it is important to be careful about what we do and have a good presence on places, like Canva, GitHub and HackerRank. We also have to do a case study to learn about things that can happen online like phishing and other dangers. The Student Digital Ambassador project wants to help students learn what they need to know to use the internet and technology in a way and feel confident when they are online.


## Module 1 – Digital Literacy Awareness

I made an infographic using Canva to help my friends understand digital literacy. This infographic also shows them some tools and how to stay safe online. I wanted to make it easy and fun for them to learn about this.

## Module 2 – Professional Online Presence

I created profiles on GitHub, LinkedIn and HackerRank to build my professional presence. On GitHub I wrote an introduction about myself my education and what I want to achieve. I took pictures of these profiles to show how I plan to use them to learn and grow both in school and, in my career.


## Module 3 – Coding & Collaboration Platforms

I worked on a coding problem on HackerRank. This was a problem that people who are new to coding can try to solve. I also made a quiz on Google Forms to help my friends learn more about computers and the internet. I took pictures of my work. Added links so you can see what I did. These platforms are really helpful for learning and working with people.

## Module 4 – Professional Communication & Social Media

I wrote two emails like a professional. One email was to a teacher. The other was to someone who helps people find internships. I also made a list of things to do. Not to do on social media. This module really helped me understand how to communicate and be polite on the internet.

## Module 5 – Cybercrime Awareness

I looked into a case of cybercrime like when someone tries to trick you into giving them your password. I made a report about this case and created a checklist to help people stay safe online. This checklist has tips, on how to keep your money safe and what to do if you think someone is trying to trick you. I also found out how to report these kinds of problems to the people.

This portfolio not only demonstrates my practical digital skills but also serves as a useful guide for peers to use digital tools responsibly, build professional profiles, and stay protected from online threats.
